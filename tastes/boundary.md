# Taste: boundary

Foreground where a claim holds and where it breaks — scope conditions,
assumptions, generalization limits, failure modes. When you read a source, pull
out the conditions under which its result is true and the conditions it never
tested. Ask what would make the finding fail. Surface tensions where one work's
result is another work's out-of-scope case, and keep claims inside the
population, method, and context that earned them.

This shapes emphasis only — not page structure, support requirements, or
evidence discipline.
