# Task: polish

Proofread what the user names in `notes/` — a whole note or a passage within
one. This is the only operation that edits the human's surface, and only ever on
request.

Polish is proofreading, not rewriting. The note is its author's own retrieval
surface; their phrasing is how they find their way back into it.

1. Read the note in full, even when the target is one passage — the surrounding
   text is what tells you its terms and register.
2. Within the named scope, fix typos, grammar, and punctuation; sharpen a phrase
   toward academic register where the sentence supports it. Sentence-level only
   — meaning, claims, and the author's voice stay intact.
3. Anything structural — reordering the narrative, merging or cutting passages —
   is proposed and waits for a yes, each time.
4. Report what you changed, briefly and completely. A clean note gets `NO_OP:
   nothing to fix`.

Never add wiki conventions (frontmatter, `(Work: …)` markers, template
sections). Conversation history tells you what the user wants; it is never a
source of new content for the note. A note enters the wiki the same way a paper
does: the user says to ingest it — polish never does.

No binding floor: the note is not a wiki page and makes no claims the floor
could check. Nothing under `wiki/` is touched.
